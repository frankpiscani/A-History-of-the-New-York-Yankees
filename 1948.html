1903-1919
The Early Years

1901: A Prehistory

In 1901, their first season, the Orioles had a 68–65 win–loss record and finished in fifth place in the AL. During the season, there were numerous disputes between Johnson and McGraw over disciplinary issues, which continued into the following year. Rumors began to spread that Johnson was interested in relocating the team to New York City, in an attempt to compete directly with the NL.

1903: A move to New York

The ballpark for the New York team was constructed between 165th and 168th Streets, on Broadway in Manhattan.
